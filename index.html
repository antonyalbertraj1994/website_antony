I am pursuing my Ph.D. in Human Computer Interaction at the University of Waterloo advised by Daniel Vogel and Omid Abari. My research interests include Human Computer Interaction, Internet of Things, energy harvesting system, and haptics. Before starting my Ph.D. , I completed my Masters in Electrical and Computer Engineering from the University of Toronto and Bachelors in Electrical and Electronics Engineering from VIT, Chennai. I worked in Dynamics Graphics Project (DGP) with Aakar Gupta and Ravin Balakrishnan. During my time at DGP lab, I worked on using haptics for learning semaphoric finger gestures and investigated squeeze sensations using shape memory alloys. My Ph.D. research topics focusses on designing building reconfigurable physical displays. Reconfigurable displays are displays that allows user to customize and build displays of arbitrary shape, size, and resolution using physical pixels. Reconfigurable displays trades the fidelity of high-resolution display for flexibility in configurations. I love building wearable system. My masters final project was a wearable vibro-acoustic helmet for the visually impaired system supervised by Steve Mann.

I am hardware tinkerer and electronics hobbyist. Apart from my research publications, I have worked on other projects during my master and Bachelors, and just for fun. Please check out of the Projectssection.
